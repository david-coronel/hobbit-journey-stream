The Last Homely House

The valley of Rivendell opened before them like a secret kept by the mountains. Waterfalls sang in the distance, and the air smelled of pine and old magic. Elrond stood at the threshold of the Last Homely House, his eyes holding the memory of ages.

"Welcome," he said, and the word felt like a shield against the wilderness beyond. Inside, lamps burned with a silver flame, and tapestries told stories older than the Shire itself. Bilbo found himself both comforted and slightly intimidated — a common feeling in the presence of elves.

That evening they spoke of many things: of the journey ahead, of the dangers of the Misty Mountains, and of the swords that Gandalf and Thorin carried. Elrond read the runes upon them and named them — Orcrist and Glamdring — swords forged in Gondolin for the Goblin-wars.

Thorin’s heart swelled with pride. Bilbo’s swelled with anxiety. But both, in their own way, felt the weight of destiny pressing closer with every passing hour.
