📊 What This Reveals

Most of the book is "scene" paragraphs (62.8%), but these cluster on specific days. For example, Chapter I (The Unexpected Party) all happens in one evening - every paragraph is a "scene". But Chapter XIX covers 6 months of return journey with scattered scene paragraphs separated by huge time gaps.

The yellow segments show where narrative moments actually exist. The gaps between them represent time that passes in the story but isn't described in detail - "they marched for many days", "weeks passed", etc.

🎯 Why This Matters

The Hobbit has 1,814 paragraphs but they don't evenly cover the 14-month timeline. Only about 25 days have actual "scene" narrative, while the other 431 days exist only as time-skip summaries or are entirely skipped.

This creates a sparse narrative structure - dense clusters of action separated by wide temporal gaps. This visualization makes that structure visible.
